## What to Look for in a Prepaid SIM Plan

Before comparing plans, it is important to understand the key factors that separate a good deal from a great one.

### Data Allowance

Most people need at least 5GB of data per month for basic use, including social media, streaming, and navigation. Heavy users should look for plans offering 15GB or more, or even unlimited options.

### Network Coverage

A cheap plan means nothing if you cannot get a signal where you live and work. Look for carriers that use reliable nationwide networks. Nexitel, for example, operates on major US networks to provide coast-to-coast coverage without the premium price tag.

### International Features

If you travel or call abroad, look for plans that include international calling or affordable roaming add-ons. This is an area where many prepaid carriers fall short, but providers like Nexitel include international calling to select countries on many plans.

### No Hidden Fees

One of the biggest advantages of prepaid is transparency. The price you see should be the price you pay, with no activation fees, no credit checks, and no early termination penalties.

## Prepaid vs Postpaid: The Numbers

The average American spends over $100 per month on a postpaid wireless plan when you factor in device payments and fees. Switching to a prepaid plan can save anywhere from $300 to $700 per year, depending on your usage.

With prepaid, you also avoid credit checks, long-term commitments, and the frustration of hidden fees appearing on your bill. You pay for exactly what you need, and you can change or cancel your plan at any time.
